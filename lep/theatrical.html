Tips/Tricks/Advice

Do your baking a couple of days before, cook your meats the day before, and start your stew that morning if you’re making it from scratch. The idea is to make it so you can so you can relax and enjoy the movies!

Designate a food staging areas where you can quickly grab your foods. We typically break the foods down into cold, hot, and room temp. Room temp foods get put out on a table, hot foods get stored in a crockpot, and cold get stored in a cooler

Use crockpots to keep your hot foods hot. That way you can keep the food close to hand and not have to keep running to the kitchen. They’re pretty easy to pick up at thrift stores.

Wrap your meats in aluminum foil. This makes it easy to heat them in a crockpot, and easy to hand out to your fellowship.

Use #10 cans and ice packs as personal mini coolers to keep your drinks cold.

Keep your portions small, there’s a lot of food to be eaten.

There are times when characters are shown holding a bowl or cup of something and there’s just no way to determine what it actually is, so we guess, and that’s what will be shown in the list of foods. Typically, if it’s a bowl we’ll do stew, if it’s a mug, depending on the scene, either ale or tea.

Recipes

Vile Fish Stew

  • 1 can tuna (undrained)
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 celery stalks (sliced)
  • random seasonings
  • heat on stove, sniff, throw away

Lembas (Basic Shortbread)

  • 1 cup salted butter (softened)
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar
  • 1 1/2 cups flour
  • Cream butter and sugar. Add flour. Roll out dough and cut shapes. Line baking sheet with aluminum foil. Bake at 300oF for 8 minutes or until bottom of cookie is just barely turning golden. Feel free to add flavorings and/or nuts.
